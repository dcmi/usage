### BIBO "Contribution" class

- https://github.com/dcmi/dc-srap/issues/97 "April model"
- https://github.com/dcmi/dc-srap/issues/100 - Property names for use with Contribution instances (Tom)

Niklas: Tom proposed definitions yesterday and some possible adjustments to that. I think we are approaching consensus, would you agree?

Karen: As usual, I consent the least. I suggested alternative definitions to the very terse definitions that just repeat the word "contribution", for example. Terse definitions would need comments to clarify. Also, rather "agent that" instead of "agent who" since it might not be a person.

Tom agrees that comments and explanation are needed. "Agent that" is good.

Niklas: I would prefer terse definitions plus more elaborate comments. But that is just personal preference.
Also, we can work it out in more detail when we make the actual additions to BIBO. But we need a very concrete structure to do that so that the CR is not too wobbly.

Karen: I am fine with terse definitions but just using the same terms is not a definition! "Contribution" as such needs to be defined.

Phil: I agree, it does not look good. We need a thesaurus, haha. People look at the definition bc they do not understand the label and want to check what it means.

Tom: We have gotten away for twenty years without defining contribution but I see the point. Using the same words shows how they are related. Someone convinced me that contribution and hasContribution are really the same.

Niklas: I see the point. But this is good bc it means that we agree to add these terms, it is just about the definitions. Use skos:scopeNote? We agree on the general direction. Relation from resource to contribution should be called qualifiedContribution or just contribution or hasContribution?

Osma: synonyms are "participation, involvement, activity, impact, influence"

Karen: https://github.com/dcmi/dc-srap/issues/100#issuecomment-4537854571 were some of my suggestions

Karen: Whichever one we choose, I don't feel they need to be the same

Niklas: To make it crystal clear we might have to call it contributionSomething or even contributionAgentRole but I think that would be overkill.

Phil: I was thinking about how contribution might be misunderstood. One interpretation might be that someone wrote a chapter, relationship to hasPart -- contribution being an actual subset of the resource -- this is not what is meant, rather what was their role in doing it. I don't want to knock us back to stage one but do we actually have a contributorProfile here?

Niklas: qualifiedAttribution? we discussed this last time and did not feel that it sold the problem

Karen: people rejected contributor bc could be confused with DC terms contributor. I agree that we are profiling the contributor. The agent definition is the primary, and role and affiliation are kind of secondary. To me the contribution is the agent and how the agent relates. We created this box called contribution but I am not sure that that is going to resonate with people.

Niklas: It is probably going to resonate with the Bibframe people. I wonder how to go from here without backtracking too much.

Karen: When I was working with definitions I had another question. Do we want to distinguish between contributions of creating the content vs being the publisher?

Niklas: I am leaning on Bibframe again, there are subcategories for that. The OWL part of me can imagine structures to entail one from the other.
The basis for all of this is still DC terms contributor and creator which is a subproperty (?), so here it is clear what that means. I still think we should try to use contributor and/or contribution. What about contributorProfile? We would probably not want to say contributorProfileAffiliation.

Phil: contributorAffiliation?

Osma: We are now on very different territory than where we started out at the beginning of the meeting, a bit frustrating -- should we rein it in?
In the beginning we toyed with notions like affiliatedAgent, just a person wearing a hat (as a metaphor).

Phil: was not my intention to push back against the April model, just put some points on what we need to explain in supplementary material

Karen: I understand wanting to be connected to Bibframe but that is a very different environment where people get training. We are thinking of SRAP in an environment where people may just pick it up and use it, is not a single community. People are coming to this cold, what would they need?

Niklas: We alll still want to have the April model. Would somebody misuse contribution, is that a real risk or can we manage with good explanations?

Phil: way forward could be to come up with a description, an explanation what a contribution actually is -- but I am not volunteering

Karen: We could expand the definitions that I started bc they don't mention contribution.

Phil: those a little bit abstract, a bit wide in their scope

Karen: I was trying to stay terse but when we are expanding we don't have to be. How would we explain it to a human being?

Niklas: I like contribution as a subtype of influence (as an aside).

Karen: Do we need a new issue for discussing contribution as a word?

Niklas: I was hoping that #97 would encompass that. And #100 is more about the naming and the details. What would the new issue do? refine the April model?

Karen: It would be to see if we could come up with a better name.

Phil: Starting from Karen's definitions, we should maybe start with "relating to" -- what sort of "relatings" would there be?

Niklas: We are describing the resulting resource. Thinking about the contribution as "the thing added". The bibliographic aspect is central to BIBO.

Karen: I commented somewhere that, will resource include things like datasets? I am still uneasy with scholarly resources, or rather, academic articles but you also have to be able to explain where the results come from so we cannot limit ourselves to just documents.

Niklas: "document" is already very overloaded anyway. In schema.org dataset is a subclass of creativeWork. So I think it is wide enough.

[Tom enters the meeting.]

Karen: Where does all the "qualified" come from?

Tom: I got that from design patterns in PROV-O. I think we need to cite related work. Whether "qualifiedContribution" makes sense I don't know. I think this is the way Wikidata uses the notion of qualification. Might be a good way to emphasize the meta nature of this construct. Somebody (Niklas) convinced me that we do not necessarily need "hasX", "X" seems to suffice.

Karen: That describes the question, now we need consensus on the name. 

Niklas: I am partial to "qualifiedContribution" bc it instroduces the casing. Also "qualified" differentiates from "reified".

Karen: That is RDF jargon that not many people have a mental picture for. Not helpful, will jar people a bit. We need to speak a language that people understand.

Osma: BIBO already has chapter as a property and chapter as a class.

Niklas: and chapter is even a data property for chapter number

Tom: Do we have consensus on everything except for the name of contribution?

Karen: not very

Niklas: not a wide disagreement but we have shaken up the sense of convergence that we had. I am back to the April model. I was a bit shaken by the partOf thought but it doesn't scare me too much.

Phil: I did not intend to push against the April model. I was just sommenting on what people might associate with contribution.

Niklas: We really need to ensure that the definitions don't allow for this misinterpretations.

Karen: I think we are within the April model, it is just a matter of definitions and names of properties and classes. I can open up an issue and play with the wording. We need to have a clear idea of what we mean by contribution. Do we want to limit it to actions that went into the creation of the resource. If we are fine with that then there are other terms that we can use.

Niklas: What would we exclude?

Karen: For example people paying money. Creation is more concrete than contribution.

Osma: The scope should be exactly the same than scope of existing DC terms contributor. The intent is still the same.

Phil: Should we work in the idea of intellectual content as opposed to more mechanistic aspects?

Argie: What about data collection?

Phil: Organizing a focus group would be data collection and therefore a contribution (but not participating in the focus group). But we cannot concentrate too much about details of various disciplines.

Tom: Original definition (?) was about creating the intellectual content.
... (?)

Niklas: I tried Osma's proposed words and this came out --> "A contribution is a participation, involvement, activity, impact, and/or influence on the creation of the intellectual content of the resource." ?

Phil: maybe drop influence, it is very broad (and then the proposition works better)

Osma [in chat, about what Tom referred to]: From RFC2413:
4.2. Author or Creator Label: "Creator"
The person or organization primarily responsible for creating the intellectual content of the resource. For example, authors in the case of written documents, artists, photographers, or illustrators in the case of visual resources.

4.6. Other Contributor Label: "Contributor"
A person or organization not specified in a Creator element who has made significant intellectual contributions to the resource but whose contribution is secondary to any person or organization specified in a Creator element (for example, editor, transcriber, and illustrator).

Karen: What do we do in the definition and what in the (extended) comment? We don't want the definition this wordy. Condense this to get the definition.

Tom: We are creating properties for BIBO for SRAP, maybe we just need to make the pattern, give it names and definitions that fits the scopes of BIBO and SRAP and not solve the problem for DCMI metadata terms as a whole.

Niklas: Yeah, we cannot base what we do here on DC terms contributor in general, that is not what we want. But we want to base it on DC terms contributor nonetheless.

Tom: Wanted to use the words that were used to define DC terms contributor. A bit vague but in the context of SRAP can be clarified with examples.

Niklas: Subproperty between creator and contributor rules out many misinterpretations.

Karen: Do we have an action / issue about wording?

Tom: Issue #100 is about the names, so also about wording, we could continue in that thread.

Phil: Is the class included as well as the properties? Tom: Yes, it is in there, just not in the title. I will update that. [Niklas edits it.]

Niklas: We will continue tinkering with the April model as we have it in #100. And use the minutes here as inspiration to create our definitions.

Osma: Who volunteers to wordsmith so that we have something to discuss?

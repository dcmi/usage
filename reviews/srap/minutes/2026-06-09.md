Tom: came up with two properties, one that is a summary and one for use with conept URIs; National Library of Sweden has taken schema.org properties and turned them into concept schemes. We should come out with a proposal that we approve of and put it out for public comment in the second half of June.
What I want to discuss today is, is this roughly something that we can put out for public comment, and is public comment what we want to do?

Karen: I commented in the issue bc I am concerned about defining a DC property as having to have an IRI value, no other properties are defined that way. And I think we should think about the difference between what is defined in DC and how you can use it in a profile. Most of it would be in the profile rather than in the vocabulary.

Kalli: Could we call that out in the public comment proposal, that this would be something new? or does that open a can of worms?

Karen: no can of worms but will only get good comments from people if they are deeply aware of the philosophy of DC terms; others will just say it is great to have both

Tom: The intention was not to have one property for the IRI and another in free text, but the free text one would be a true summary and not about controlled vocabularies. There is not expectation that the two would be linked.

Tom: I still think it would be a good thing to do that, all we can do is say how it is intended to be used.

Karen: You can do that with rangeIncludes and a comment?
